## Analysis / What This Tells Us

1. **Tent-SIZE-specific queries have real volume** (40x60, 20x40, 20x30, 20x20 all show `>100` or `<100` with real KD ratings, not zero). We already capture this granularity in `listing_pricing.item_type` (e.g. `tent_10x10`, `tent_40x60`) — currently buried in the raw pricing table on listing pages, not surfaced as its own content. → Master plan item 5.

2. **"How much does X cost" question phrasing repeats identically across every category** (tent/party/table/bounce house) — this exact phrasing should be used verbatim in FAQ sections for featured-snippet targeting, not paraphrased. → Master plan item 4.

3. **Bounce house rental cost has real head-term volume (`>100`) and Easy KD** — better opportunity than tables/chairs. We already have real bounce-house pricing captured from extraction. → Master plan item 6.

4. **"Near me" intent is huge but competitive**: `party rental near me` alone is `>1000` volume but Hard KD — unrealistic to out-rank Google's local pack for the bare head term. The specific-type "near me" long-tails (pool/foam/yacht party rental near me) show Easy/Medium KD with real volume, but those are off-niche for us (not our business type). Still, confirms "near me" phrasing matters for intent — worth testing in titles/H1s, which currently don't include it at all.

5. **Table/chair-only searches are consistently thin** (`<100` volume on every single variant across two separate keyword pulls) — tents (and bounce houses) are the real demand driver in this niche, not tables/chairs alone. → Master plan item 7 (de-prioritize, don't remove).

6. **Charlotte and Indianapolis show "Hard" KD** on their own city+tent-rental terms — real signal that competition already exists in our two most-established metros. Jacksonville shows no KD data yet (newer/thinner competition there) — might be the easier metro to push rank on first.

7. **Feature-modified tent searches are a distinct pattern**: heated, air conditioned, clear top, clear tent, sperry tent. If our real captured pricing data includes these tent sub-types (some listings do, per earlier spot-checks), worth surfacing as filterable/taggable content rather than generic "tent" line items.

8. **"Average [X] rental cost" is its own repeated query pattern** across tent and bounce house categories — we already show a price range in city-page intros; could formalize as a labeled, explicit stat callout matching this phrasing.
